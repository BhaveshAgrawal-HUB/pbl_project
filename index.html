Problem Statement

Thousands of patients experience unexpected side effects due to long-term medications. While medicines are meant to heal, improper dosage and individual body differences can lead to adverse outcomes.

Current systems only list possible side effects but fail to predict which patient might experience them. There is a critical need for a proactive, data-driven system to assess individual risk before treatment begins.

Current Challenge

Challenge: Inability to proactively identify high-risk patients.

Consequence: Side effects are detected only after manifestation, leading to delayed clinical decisions.

Goal: Develop a predictive system that analyzes patient-specific data to forecast adverse effects and enable preventive healthcare.

Project Objectives

  • • Build a predictive model for medicine side effects using patient-specific data.
  • • Integrate preprocessing, feature engineering, and classification into one pipeline.
  • • Provide interpretable visualization highlighting high-risk drugs and patients.
  • • Contribute toward AI-assisted preventive healthcare.
